Automated continuous condition monitoring of industrial electrical machines to identify internal faults has become one of the critical research areas for the past decade. Among various defects, early-stage identification of insulation failure in stator winding is of notable demand as it often occurs and accounts for 37% of the overall motor failures. Identifying the current signature at its embryonic stage will effectively improve industrial machineryâ€™s downtime and repair costs. Recent advances in computational performance and sensor technology concede advanced systems for achieving these goals. The design of an AI-based fault signature analyzer (FSA) has been developed in this paper. FSA uses real-time stator current data in the time and frequency domain from healthy and faulty induction motors to train the various AI-based machine learning classifiers to identify health conditions using wavelets. Comparing machine learning algorithms such as artificial neural network, random forest, fuzzy logic, neuro-fuzzy logic, K-nearest neighbors is performed, and various performance attributes are quantified. A reliable, automatic fault signature from a motor current is thus analyzed using the fusion of a wavelet-based feature extraction technique and a capable knowledge-based efficient artificial intelligence (AI) approach.

Introduction
Three-phase induction machines are primarily the workhorses of most manufacturing industries. Therefore, induction machines are vital electromechanical devices used for various applications ranging from fans to large-scale manufacturing [1]. The machineâ€™s unstable operating conditions, such as a sudden load shift, misalignment, and insufficient maintenance, result in a shortened lifetime, inevitably leading to substantial monetary losses and inefficiencies. It is challenging to diagnose stator turnâ€“turn fault in an industrial machine during its working condition, as unscheduled motor downtime causes losses in production. When a stator turnâ€“turn failure occurs, very high currents flow in the short-circuited coil, causing isolation deterioration and fault dissemination. The stator turnâ€“turn fault has an indistinct effect on the motorâ€™s performance in the early stages. However, if not detected at its embryonic stage, it can result in phase-to-phase or phase-to-ground short-circuitsâ€™ fault. Faults are unacceptable and thus must be witnessed to prevent all these failures [2, 3].

According to the industry standard, most of the failures are introduced by the stator, rotor, and bearings. A similar survey was carried out by the IEEE Industrial Applications Society and the Electric Power Research Institute (EPRI); insulation failure in the stator winding accounts for more than 37% of overall industrial machinesâ€™ failures [4,5,6]. Therefore, the current research has designed and developed an AI-based FSA for the automated monitoring of stator winding insulation failures in the industrial system in real-time conditions. Preventive and corrective are primary aspects of conventional maintenance methods. Most manufacturing industries are currently dependent on preventive maintenance, which operates before a breakdown occurs. AI-based fault signature analyzer
The conventional fault detection and diagnostic techniques use various motor signals such as voltage [35], flux and instant power [21], vibration [22], acoustic signal [36], temperature [23], current signature, electromagnetic or mechanical torque for continuous automated monitoring [37]. Vibration analysis is one of the most popular methods used in industrial motor condition monitoring. The vibration technique takes data from an accelerometer (to measure the casing vibrations) and eddy current transducers that monitor the rotating shafts directly to measure the radial shaft displacement [38]. The main problem with vibration analysis is that it needs costly expertise in condition monitoring and relevant experience. The vibration data are prone to error due to its impulsive nature but performs better with a stationary signal. In temperature-based analysis, the temperature is used as an indicator for identifying faults using thermography techniques. Suppose electrical equipment works in healthy conditions for a longer period in severe conditions due to power requirements. In that case, the motor may recognize it as a failure due to the temperature difference, although the motor runs in a healthy condition.

Nevertheless, the stator current signature is used in practice due to its noninvasive nature [28, 39]. Various studies have shown that artificial neural network can identify patterns and the fault current signature compared to any other machine learning algorithms with a greater identification accuracy [40,41,42]. An artificial neural network is among the most widely used classifiers for fault diagnosis. Therefore, a reliable, automatic fault signature from a motor current is analyzed using a fusion of wavelet-based feature extraction technique with a knowledge-based artificial neural network is shown in Fig. 1.

Wavelets
Wavelet transform uses a window that can be used to translate or scale the requisite signal. Wavelet uses space-based functions, both real and Fourier. Wavelets can approximate high frequencies with smaller time-domain windows and allow a proportionally larger window for lower frequencies. Wavelet can have excellent time resolution for high-frequency signals and adequate frequency resolution for low-frequency signals; the fusion of both constraints is ideal for real-time applications. Continuous wavelet transformation, or CWT, is used to analyze time-frequencies, and discrete wavelet transformation, or DWT, is used to denoise a signal. The proposed work uses discrete wavelet transformation over continuous wavelet transformation. The number of coefficients obtained after the transformation is the same as the input sample, and no data are lost. CWT contains many coefficients, but most are redundant, resulting in computationally inefficient work with continuous wavelet transformation coefficients. Haar wavelet, Daubechies wavelet, Symlets, Coiflets, biorthogonal wavelet, reverse biorthogonal wavelet, and discrete Meyer wavelet are the various types of wavelets available for discrete wavelet transformation. The proposed AI-based fault signature analyzer for real-time condition monitoring for industrial machines uses several wavelet decomposition levels to identify internal failures at their incipient stages.

Pattern recognition
Any pattern recognition problem essentially comprises feature extraction, feature selection, and classification to recognize the signature of fault from both healthy and faulty motors. The next step, DWT, is to obtain wavelet coefficients and take statistical features to train classifier models from the obtained coefficients. The wavelet-based function extraction significantly reduces the computational complexity compared to training the classifier models with the raw stator current data. To reduce the number of input variables is prudent to both lessen the modeling computational costs and, in some cases, improve the modelâ€™s performance. Hence, the selection of statistical features from the waveletâ€™s coefficients uses a decision tree algorithm. The decision tree is a tree-based learning methodology used to represent classification rules. A conventional decision tree induced with the C4.5 algorithm consists of one root, several branches, nodes, and many leaves. Each node includes one attribute, and one branch is a chain of nodes from the root to a leaf. A feature in the tree provides learning about the significance of the associated attribute [48]. J48 algorithm (WEKA implementation of C4.5) is a generally used one to build the decision tree. The machine learning model then used the best prominent selected features from the decision tree. The proposed work uses different machine learning models, such as neuro-fuzzy logic, random forest, K-nearest neighbor, fuzzy logic, artificial neural network, to classify stator winding faults in industrial machines. These machine learning models are compared in detail with statistical features and without them. Finally, an efficient model was selected using an AI-based FSA to give the extensive fault signature under various operating conditions such as loading and fault stipulations.
