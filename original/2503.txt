This paper considers the stabilization of stochastic delayed networks with Markovian switching via aperiodically intermittent control (AIC). The concepts of average control ratio and average control period are proposed to characterize the distributions of control and rest intervals of AIC. It should be noted that the averaging technique used here is more general and less restrictive than the quasi-periodicity condition and minimum control ratio condition used in previous works. Then two kinds of stabilization criteria are obtained: (1) the upper bound of time delay should be less than the average control width; (2) the upper bound of time delay has no relationship with the average control width. Finally, the results are applied to studying the stabilization of coupled stochastic neural networks with Markovian switching via AIC. Numerical simulations are provided to show the effectiveness of obtained results.

Introduction
Over the past decade, much effort has been dedicated to investigating the dynamics of complex networks due to their wide applications in different fields [1, 37, 40]. It should be noted that networks in real-world applications are often subjected to stochastic perturbations, which may be caused by external random fluctuations or other random factors in the process of transmission [14, 29, 39, 42, 43, 50]. For the constructive role of noise in nonlinear dynamical systems, please refer to [9] for details. On the other hand, in order to model the abrupt variations in systemsâ€™ structure, Markovian switching systems have also received much attention [32]. In many situations, stochastic perturbations and Markovian switching phenomena are found existing in one process simultaneously [30, 48]. Therefore, it is of great interest to study the dynamics, like stability, synchronization, eta., of stochastic complex networks with Markovian switching. Some related results have been reported (see, e.g., [17, 21]).

In recent years, a kind of discontinuous control named intermittent control has been widely used for the stabilization and synchronization of networks since this control strategy can reduce the cost and prolong the controllerâ€™s working life. In existing works, the intermittent control is mainly classed into the event/state-driven intermittent control (the controller is activated when a pre-given event occurs) [7, 12] and time-driven intermittent control (the controller is activated at pre-specified time intervals) [2, 24, 31, 52]. As for the time-driven intermittent control, according to the pre-specified time intervals, it can be classified into periodically intermittent control (PIC) [2, 8, 31] and aperiodically intermittent control (AIC) [24, 52]. In [26,27,28], Liu and Chen first studied the synchronization of coupled networks with and without time delay via AIC. Then based on [26,27,28], various extensions have been made for the stabilization or synchronization of different types of coupled networks via AIC. For example, in [3], based on a matrix omega-measure approach, the synchronization of switched complex networks with unstable modes was investigated via AIC. In [11], the synchronization of neural networks with reaction-diffusion terms via AIC was investigated. In [19], the stabilization analysis of coupled hybrid stochastic networks with multiple delays via AIC was carried out. It should be noted that in above references about the time-driven AIC, the quasi-periodicity condition (see, e.g., [3, 19, 27, 45, 49]) or the minimum control ratio condition (see, e.g., [11, 27, 44, 45, 53]) is imposed to characterize the aperiodicity of AIC. Actually, the two constraints are still conservative and may not satisfy the requirements in many real applications. For example, wind power generation is typically aperiodically intermittent and is completely irregular. It is usually unreasonable to require the length of each wind time. But we can approximately obtain the average length of wind over a unit time [47]. Therefore, it may be more practical to describe the distribution of control and rest intervals of AIC from the average sense, which will make the results more effective for wider applications.

On the other hand, it is well known that time delay is often encountered in many practical systems [41]. The existence of time delay may result in undesired dynamics. Ignoring them may bring about incorrect analytical conclusions. So, it is necessary to take time delay into account when investigating the stability of stochastic networks. In [3, 19, 45, 49], the authors considered the stabilization or synchronization of coupled stochastic networks with time-varying delay via AIC, respectively, where the upper bound of the time-varying delay was required to be less than the lower bound of all control widths. In [11, 45], no restriction was imposed on the upper bound of time-varying delay and the control (and/or rest) widths of AIC. In [27], both the two types of time delay were studied, but the quasi-periodicity condition or the minimum control ratio condition mentioned in above paragraph was imposed.

Motivated by above discussions, this paper aims to investigate the stabilization problem of stochastic delayed networks with Markovian switching via AIC from the average viewpoint. Two concepts, average control ratio and average control period, are proposed to characterize the aperiodicity of AIC. Then several novel stabilization criteria are given for â€˜smallâ€™ time delay and â€˜largeâ€™ time delay, respectively, where the scale of â€˜smallâ€™ or â€˜largeâ€™ time delay is measured by the average control interval instead of the lower bound of all control intervals. For the case of â€˜smallâ€™ time delay, it does not require the upper bound of â€˜smallâ€™ time delay to be less than the lower bound of control widths. For the case of â€˜smallâ€™ time delay, it does not require the upper bound of â€˜smallâ€™ time delay to be less than the lower bound of control widths. Actually, this paper can be seen as an extension of recent paper [46] from the single system to the coupling networks.

Conclusions
This paper studied the stabilization of stochastic delayed networks with Markovian switching via AIC from an average viewpoint. The concepts of average control ratio and average control period were proposed to characterize the distribution of control and rest intervals of AIC. It was worth noting that the AIC in this paper removed the strict restrictions on the maximum proportion of rest intervals or the lower bound of control intervals and upper bound of control periods that required in the classical AIC in previous literature. Hence, the conservatism is reduced compared with the existing works on AIC. In this paper, the intermittent control was activated synchronously. In our future work, we will consider the asynchronously intermittent control. Besides, in many applications, it is shown that noise can enhance the stability (see [10, 34]), in the future, we will pay more attention to the stabilization procedure due to the noise.
